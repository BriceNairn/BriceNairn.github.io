<h3>Overview</h3>

This demonstration project showcases a typical property impact assessment workflow used to support road widening and transport corridor planning projects.

Using a hypothetical widening alignment, the exercise demonstrates how GIS can be used to identify affected parcels, assess dwelling impacts, calculate land-take requirements and communicate findings through a professional map series.

While the data and alignment shown are illustrative, the workflow, analysis techniques and outputs reflect the type of work commonly undertaken during early-stage transport planning and infrastructure investigations.

<h3>Objectives</h3>
The project was designed to demonstrate:

- Corridor impact assessment
- Cadastral analysis
- Building footprint impact identification
- Land acquisition estimation
- Engineering and stakeholder mapping outputs
- Multi-sheet map series production

<h3>Methodology</h3>
A proposed road widening corridor was overlaid against cadastral boundaries and dwelling footprints. Impacted parcels were identified and the area of land affected within each lot was calculated.

The resulting information was presented as a review-ready map series designed to support planning and engineering discussions.
