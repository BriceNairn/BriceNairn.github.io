**Role:** Enterprise Application Specialist (GIS/CAD)

**Platform:** Sharepoint

**Tools:** Figma

**Focus Areas:** UX Design, Information Architecture, Internal Platforms


As part of a broader initiative to modernise internal GIS and CAD experiences at SA Power Networks, I designed a high-level wireframe in Figma for the Digital Engineering Applications (DEA) Hub. The objective was to improve application discoverability, streamline support pathways, and establish a scalable information architecture for a growing suite of engineering applications.

<h3>Project Overview</h3>
    The DEA hub consolidates GIS and CAD capabilities into a single SharePoint-based interface. My task was to produce a lightweight wireframe exploring layout, hierarchy, and user flow before moving into full UI design. The focus was clarity: users should immediately grasp what DEA offers and be able to access tools or request support in two clicks or fewer.

<h3>Design Approach</h3>
    In Figma, I structured the page into three clear content zones: 

<h4>Introductory Hero Section</h4>
    Establishes the DEA identity with a clean title block and a prominent action button linking to the team’s calendar. 

<h4>Informational Content Blocks</h4>
    These sections provide context around the DEA team's purpose, helping users understand how GIS and CAD services support engineering and operational outcomes before interacting with applications.

<h4>Application Cards & Support Actions</h4>
    I designed modular card components representing the main tool categories: GIS Applications, CAD & Autodesk, Map Products, and the DEA Roadmap. Below that, a second set of cards directs users to request access, log issues, or submit improvement ideas—core actions for internal stakeholders. 
    This modular layout supports scalability, consistency, and quick iteration. Components can easily be re-skinned or expanded as the platform grows. 

<h3>Outcomes</h3>
    The wireframe provided a common reference point for discussions between GIS, CAD, and engineering stakeholders, helping align expectations around navigation, content hierarchy, and support workflows before development commenced.

By establishing these foundations early, the team was able to identify improvements, reduce ambiguity during design discussions, and move into prototyping with greater confidence.
